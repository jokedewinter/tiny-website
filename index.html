Some sillyness

My work explores the relationship between acquired synesthesia and life as performance.

With influences as diverse as Kafka and Francis Bacon, new tensions are synthesised from both mundane and transcendant meanings.

Ever since I was a teenager I have been fascinated by the essential unreality of meaning. What starts out as yearning soon becomes manipulated into a hegemony of futility, leaving only a sense of unreality and the possibility of a new beginning.

As wavering phenomena become distorted through emergent and repetitive practice, the viewer is left with a hymn to the darkness of our condition.
